**Latest Requirements for Foreigners Applying for Chinese Visas (Updated October 2025)**

2025-10-11 20:00

The Chinese Consulate General in Cebu will officially launch its online visa application service on October 13, 2025. From that date, applicants can enjoy the convenience of completing forms and uploading supporting documents online through the China Online Visa Application website. After the online application is approved, applicants can proceed to submit their physical documents at the Consulate's Visa Hall. (Applicants for diplomatic, official, and courtesy visas should still submit their applications at the Chinese Embassy in Manila, Philippines.)

Please note: The Chinese Consulate General in Cebu will no longer accept Chinese visa application forms completed before October 11, 2025, starting from October 13, 2025.

**II. Required Documents**

**Basic Application Materials**

(1) Visa Application Form; (Printed on A4 paper)

(2) Passport: Original and photocopy of the bio-data page. An original passport valid for more than 6 months with blank visa pages, plus one photocopy of the passport's photo/information page. Please also provide all previously held old passports. Ôºõ

(3) One recent photo (taken within the last 6 months, front view, without a hat, color, small 2-inch size: 48mm x 33mm);

(4) Copies of all previously held Chinese visas; Ôºõ

(5) Proof of legal stay or residence in the Philippines. Third-country citizens temporarily in the Philippines should apply for their Chinese visa in advance in their home country; this Consulate will not accept their applications. For third-country citizens in the Philippines: Additionally, provide a copy of the Philippine visa and entry stamp in the passport.

(6) In addition to the five basic materials above, applicants must submit the corresponding supporting documents for their specific visa category as listed in the table below:

**Visa Categories and Required Materials**

C

**C (Crew):** Issued to crew members of international trains, aircraft, and vessels, their accompanying family members, and international road transport drivers performing duties.

**Required:** A guarantee letter from the foreign transportation company or an invitation letter from a relevant Chinese entity.

D

**D (Permanent Residence):** Issued to persons entering China for permanent residence.

**Required:** The original and a copy of the "Confirmation Form for Foreigner's Permanent Residence Status" issued by the Ministry of Public Security.

F

**F (Visit/Exchange):** Issued to persons entering China for exchanges, visits, inspections, etc.

**Required:** An invitation letter issued by the inviting party in China.

G

**G (Transit):** Issued to persons transiting through China.

**Required:** Confirmed onward tickets (air, train, or ship) with specific dates and seat numbers to the destination country (region).

J1

**J1 (Resident Journalist):** Issued to foreign resident journalists of foreign news agencies permanently stationed in China.

**Required:** A visa notification letter from the Information Department of the Chinese Ministry of Foreign Affairs and an official letter from the journalist's media organization.

J2

**J2 (Short-term Journalist):** Issued to foreign journalists entering China for short-term news coverage.

**Required:** A visa notification letter from the Information Department of the Chinese Ministry of Foreign Affairs or a relevant unit, and an official letter from the journalist's media organization.

L

**L (Tourist):** Issued to persons entering China for tourism. Group L visas may be issued for group tourism.

**Required:** Travel itinerary, round-trip flight tickets, hotel reservations, etc., OR an invitation letter from a Chinese entity or individual. The invitation letter must include:

(a) Invitee's personal information: name, gender, date of birth, etc.;

(b) Invitee's travel itinerary: dates of arrival and departure, places to visit, etc.;

(c) Inviting unit or individual's information: name of inviting unit or individual, contact phone number, address, official seal of the unit, signature of the legal representative or inviting individual. A copy of the inviter's Chinese Resident ID card, or a copy of the inviter's foreign passport and Chinese residence permit.

M

**M (Business/Trade):** Issued to persons entering China for commercial and trade activities.

**Required:** An invitation letter issued by the Chinese commercial/trade partner. The invitation letter must include:

(a) Invitee's personal information: name, gender, date of birth, etc.;

(b) Invitee's visit information: purpose of visit to China, dates of arrival and departure, places to visit, relationship with the inviting unit/individual, source of funding, etc.;

(c) Inviting unit or individual's information: name of inviting unit or individual, contact phone number, address, official seal of the unit, signature of the legal representative or inviting individual.

Q1

**Q1 (Family Reunion Long-term):** Issued to family members of Chinese citizens or foreign citizens with Chinese permanent residence status applying to reside in China (over 180 days) for family reunion, and persons applying to enter and reside in China for reasons such as foster care.

**Required:** An invitation letter from the Chinese citizen or foreign permanent resident residing in China, a copy of the inviter's ID, and original and copies of proof of family relationship. For foster care applications, submit a notarized and authenticated foster care power of attorney, the passport of the entrusting party, proof of kinship, the foster care recipient's consent letter and ID copy. If one or both of the child's parents are Chinese citizens, also provide a copy of the proof of the Chinese parent(s)' residence abroad at the time of the child's birth.

Q2

**Q2 (Family Visit Short-term):** Issued to relatives of Chinese citizens residing in China or foreign citizens with Chinese permanent residence status, applying to visit China short-term (not exceeding 180 days, inclusive).

**Required:** An invitation letter from the Chinese citizen or foreign permanent resident residing in China, and a copy of the inviter's ID.

R

**R (Talent/Expert):** Issued to foreign high-level talents and urgently needed professionals required by the state.

**Required:** Meet the conditions and requirements for introducing foreign high-level talents and urgently needed professionals as determined by relevant competent Chinese government departments, and submit corresponding proof materials accordingly.

S1

**S1 (Private Affairs Long-term):** Issued to spouses, parents, children under 18, and parents-in-law of foreigners residing in China for work, study, etc., applying to visit China long-term (over 180 days), and persons needing to reside in China for other private affairs.

**Required:** An invitation letter from the foreigner residing in China for work, study, etc., original and copies of proof of family relationship, a copy of the inviter's ID (passport and residence permit); OR proof materials required for entering China to handle private affairs.

S2

**S2 (Private Affairs Short-term):** (a) Issued to family members of foreigners residing in China for work, study, etc., applying to visit China short-term (not exceeding 180 days, inclusive).

(b) Issued to persons needing to stay in China for other private affairs.

**Required:** (a) An invitation letter from the foreigner residing in China for work, study, etc., a copy of proof of family relationship, a copy of the inviter's ID (passport and residence permit);

(b) Proof materials required for handling private affairs in China.

X1

**X1 (Student Long-term):** Issued to persons applying to study in China long-term (over 180 days).

**Required:** The original and a copy of the admission notice issued by the admitting institution and the original and a copy of the "Visa Application for Study in China" form (JW-201 or JW-202) issued by the competent authority.

X2

**X2 (Student Short-term):** Issued to persons applying to study in China short-term (not exceeding 180 days, inclusive).

**Required:** The original and a copy of the admission notice issued by the admitting institution.

Z

**Z (Work):** Issued to persons applying to work in China.

**Required:** Provide one of the following proof materials:

(a) "Notification Letter of Foreigner's Work Permit" issued by the State Administration of Foreign Experts Affairs (scanned printout or fax acceptable);

(b) Copy of the "Registration Certificate of Resident Representative Office of Foreign (Regional) Enterprise" issued by the Administration for Market Regulation, or copy of the "Approval Certificate of Resident Representative Office of Foreign Enterprise" issued by the commerce department;

(c) Original and copy of the approval document for commercial cultural performances issued by the cultural administrative department;

(d) "Invitation Letter for Foreigners to Engage in Offshore Oil Operations in the People's Republic of China" issued by China National Offshore Oil Corporation (scanned printout or fax acceptable).

Applicants under 18 must provide a birth certificate and be accompanied by a guardian.

**Note:** For short-term work in China, please apply for a visa according to the "Procedures for Foreigners Entering China to Complete Short-term Work Tasks (Trial)." See the attachment for details.

The invitation letters mentioned above must include the following:

(a) Invitee's personal information: name, gender, date of birth, etc.;

(b) Invitee's visit information: purpose of visit to China, dates of arrival and departure, places to visit, relationship with the inviting unit/individual, source of funding, etc.;

(c) Inviting unit or individual's information: name of inviting unit or individual, contact phone number, address, official seal of the unit, signature of the legal representative or inviting individual.

**Proof of Family Relationship** Refers to marriage certificate, birth certificate, kinship certificate issued by a public security station, or notarized kinship certificate, etc.

**Additional Requirements**

**1. First-time Travelers to China:** In addition to the basic application materials, please provide the following based on your personal circumstances:

(a) Bank deposit statement from the last 6 months and the bank's receipt for issuing this statement;

(b) Proof of tax payment (BIR-stamped income tax return form);

(c) Employment certificate (stating income and years of service);

(d) Business registration certificate (if the applicant is self-employed);

(e) Professional ID, student ID, etc. (applicants may provide as applicable);

(f) Other materials that can prove the applicant's financial status, employment, or studies (applicants may provide as applicable).

(Note: Children, students, homemakers, etc., who cannot provide their own bank deposit statements or tax payment proofs may provide those of their parents or spouse, along with proof of relationship.) **Bank Deposit Statement and Tax Payment Proof** Ôºâ


**2. Former Chinese Passport:** Foreign nationals of Chinese descent born in China (including Hong Kong, Macao, and Taiwan) applying for a Chinese visa for the first time must provide the original of their previously held Chinese passport (including Hong Kong, Macao, and Taiwan) and photocopies of the passport's photo/information page and extension pages (if any). Bank deposit statements and tax payment proofs are not required in this case.

**3. Name Change Proof:** If the name on the new passport differs from that on the old passport, provide official documentation proving the name change.

**4. Travel to Tibet:** Applicants can travel to Tibet by joining a tour group organized by a locally authorized travel agency. For independent travel to the Tibet Autonomous Region, prior contact must be made with the Tibet Tourism Bureau (Tel: 0086-891-6834313, Fax: 0086-891-6834632), and a written notice issued by this bureau must be provided.

**5. Special Tourism Activities:** For tourists engaging in activities like hiking, cycling, horseback riding, exploration, hot air ballooning, etc., a "Tourism Invitation Letter" issued by a Chinese "Authorized Tourism Unit" is required.

(1) **Regular Processing:** Ready for pickup on the 4th working day (counting from the day the application is accepted).

* The processing timeline is calculated from the date you submit your passport in person.

**Visa Application Important Notes**

**1.** **Application materials must be truthful and complete.** The Consulate General will verify the authenticity of application materials through various channels. Providing forged or incomplete materials may result in refusal of the application and affect future applications.

**2. Maintain polite and civilized behavior during the in-person visit.** Any verbal or behavioral offense towards Consulate staff may lead to visa refusal and affect future applications.

**3. Applicants must fill out the application form truthfully, completely, and clearly.** The form must be signed by the applicant personally (minors may be signed by parents or guardians, indicating the relationship).

**4. Answer the visa officer's questions truthfully.**

**5. According to Chinese regulations and international practice, consular officers have the right to request additional supporting documents and to refuse a visa without stating reasons.**

**6. Pay the prescribed fees at the bank.** Applicants are responsible for any consequences of overpayment.

**7. When collecting the visa, the applicant is responsible for checking the visa details and should raise any questions immediately.**

**8. J1, S1, X1, and Z visas are typically valid for 3 months for a single entry.** Holders must apply for a residence permit at the local public security bureau within 30 days of entering China.

**9. C (Crew) visas are for duty purposes only.** If crew members need to stay in China for personal reasons after completing their duties or not for duty purposes, they must apply for the appropriate visa separately. Holders must enter and exit China together with their vehicle (aircraft, train, ship). Those who cannot do so must apply for the appropriate visa separately.

**10. Applicants are solely responsible for the consequences arising from the following situations; please take care to avoid them:**

(a) Visa refusal due to incomplete or untruthful information on the application form;

(b) Visa refusal due to non-compliant passport or photo, or failure to provide required materials as requested by the visa officer;

(c) Being denied boarding or entry due to applying too early (causing visa expiry before travel) or failing to obtain the visa in time;

(d) Visa becoming invalid and unusable due to the applicant's own arrangements or third-party reasons after issuance;

(e) Being denied boarding or entry due to failure to check visa details (number of entries, validity, duration of stay) and passport validity before traveling to China;

(f) Other reasons attributable to the applicant leading to the visa being unusable.
