Latest Requirements for Foreigners Applying for Chinese Visas (Updated October 2025)

2025-10-11 20:00

The Chinese Consulate General in Cebu will officially launch its online visa application service on October 13, 2025. From that date, applicants can enjoy the convenience of completing forms and uploading supporting documents online via the China Online Visa Application website. After the online application is approved, applicants can proceed to submit their physical documents at the Consulate's Visa Hall. (Applicants for diplomatic, official, courtesy visas, etc., should still submit their applications at the Chinese Embassy in Manila, Philippines.)

Please note: Starting October 13, 2025, the Chinese Consulate General in Cebu will no longer accept Chinese visa application forms completed before October 11, 2025.

I. Application Process

Following the launch of the online service, the visa application process is as follows:

Applicants must log in to the China Online Visa Application website ( https://consular.mfa.gov.cn/VISA/ ), complete the form, upload the required documents, and then personally or through a representative submit the original passport along with the corresponding supporting materials at the Consulate General. We strongly recommend visiting the website and clicking on "Visa Application Guide" for detailed procedural instructions and the specific document checklist for different visa categories. The specific steps are outlined below:

A single account can be used to submit applications for multiple individuals, with no limit on the number; each person requires a separate application and unique application number. On the homepage, click "Start My Application," select "Cebu," and then truthfully, completely, and accurately fill in all required information according to your visa category. II. Application Materials

Basic Application Materials

(1) Visa Application Form; (Printed on A4 paper)

(2) Passport: Original and a photocopy of the information page. An original passport valid for more than 6 months with blank visa pages, plus one photocopy of the passport's photo/information page. Please also provide all previously held old passports. Ôºõ

(3) One recent photo (taken within the last 6 months, front view, without a hat, color, small 2-inch size 48mm x 33mm);

(4) Photocopies of all previously held Chinese visas. Ôºõ

(5) Third-country nationals in the Philippines: Proof of legal stay or residence in the Philippines, along with photocopies of the Philippine visa and entry stamps in the passport. Third-country nationals temporarily visiting the Philippines should apply for their Chinese visa in advance in their home country; our Consulate will not accept such applications.

(6) In addition to the above five basic materials, applicants must submit the corresponding supporting materials for their specific visa category as listed in the table below:

Visa Categories and Required Materials

C

Issued to crew members of international trains, aircraft crews, seamen on international voyages and their accompanying family members, and drivers engaged in international road transportation.

Submit a guarantee letter issued by a foreign transportation company or an invitation letter issued by a relevant entity in China.

D

Issued to those entering China for permanent residence.

Submit the original and a photocopy of the "Confirmation Form for Foreigner's Permanent Residence Status" issued by the Ministry of Public Security.

F

Issued to those entering China for exchanges, visits, inspections, etc.

Submit an invitation letter issued by the inviting party in China.

G

Issued to those transiting through China.

Submit confirmed onward air (train, ship) tickets with specific dates and seats to the destination country (region).

J1

Issued to resident foreign correspondents of foreign news agencies permanently stationed in China.

Visa notification letter issued by the Information Department of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of China and an official letter from the journalist's media organization.

J2

Issued to foreign journalists entering China for short-term news coverage.

Visa notification letter issued by the Information Department of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of China or a relevant unit, and an official letter from the journalist's media organization.

L

Issued to those entering China for tourism; group L visas may be issued for group tourism.

Submit travel itinerary, round-trip flight tickets, hotel reservations, etc., or an invitation letter issued by a unit or individual in China. The invitation letter must include:

a) Invitee's personal information: name, gender, date of birth, etc.;

b) Invitee's travel itinerary: dates of arrival and departure, places to visit, etc.;

c) Inviting unit or inviter information: name of inviting unit or inviter, contact phone number, address, official seal of the unit, signature of the legal representative or inviter, etc. A photocopy of the inviter's Chinese Resident Identity Card, or a photocopy of the inviter's foreign passport and Chinese legal residence permit.

M

Issued to those entering China for commercial and trade activities.

Submit an invitation letter issued by the Chinese commercial/trade partner. The invitation letter must include:

a) Invitee's personal information: name, gender, date of birth, etc.;

b) Invitee's visit information: purpose of visit to China, dates of arrival and departure, places to visit, relationship with the inviting unit/inviter, source of funds, etc.;

c) Inviting unit or inviter information: name of inviting unit or inviter, contact phone number, address, official seal of the unit, signature of the legal representative or inviter, etc.

Q1

Issued to family members of Chinese citizens or foreign citizens with permanent residence status in China applying to reside in China for family reunion (over 180 days), and those applying to enter and reside in China for reasons such as foster care.

Submit an invitation letter issued by a Chinese citizen residing in China or a foreign citizen with permanent residence status in China, a photocopy of the inviter's ID, and original and photocopies of proof of family relationship. For foster care applications, submit a notarized and authenticated foster care power of attorney, passport of the entrusting party, proof of kinship, consent letter and ID photocopy of the foster care trustee. If one or both parents of the child are Chinese citizens, also submit a photocopy of proof of the Chinese parent(s)' residence abroad at the time of the child's birth.

Q2

Issued to those visiting relatives in China for a short term (not exceeding 180 days, inclusive), where the applicant is a relative of a Chinese citizen residing in China or a foreign citizen with permanent residence status in China.

Submit an invitation letter issued by a Chinese citizen residing in China or a foreign citizen with permanent residence status in China, and a photocopy of the inviter's ID.

R

Issued to foreign high-level talents and urgently needed professionals required by the state.

Applicants must meet the conditions and requirements for introducing foreign high-level talents and urgently needed professionals as determined by the relevant competent authorities of the Chinese government and submit corresponding proof materials accordingly.

S1

Issued to spouses, parents, children under 18 years old, and parents-in-law of foreigners residing in China for work, study, etc., applying to visit China long-term (over 180 days), and those needing to reside in China for other private affairs.

Submit an invitation letter issued by the foreigner residing in China for work, study, etc., original and photocopies of proof of family relationship, photocopy of the inviter's ID (passport and residence permit); or proof materials required for entering China to handle private affairs.

S2

a) Issued to those visiting relatives in China for a short term (not exceeding 180 days, inclusive), where the applicant is a family member of a foreigner residing in China for work, study, etc.

b) Issued to those needing to stay in China for other private affairs.

a) Submit an invitation letter issued by the foreigner residing in China for work, study, etc., a photocopy of proof of family relationship, and a photocopy of the inviter's ID (passport and residence permit);

b) Proof materials required for handling private affairs in China.

X1

Issued to those applying to study in China long-term (over 180 days).

Submit the original and a photocopy of the admission notice issued by the admitting institution and the original and a photocopy of the "Visa Application for Study in China" (Form JW-201 or JW-202) issued by the competent authority.

X2

Issued to those applying to study in China short-term (not exceeding 180 days, inclusive).

Submit the original and a photocopy of the admission notice issued by the admitting institution.

Z

Issued to those applying to work in China.

Must provide one of the following proof materials:

a) "Notification Letter of Foreigner's Work Permit" issued by the State Administration of Foreign Experts Affairs (scanned printout or fax copy acceptable);

b) Photocopy of "Registration Certificate of Resident Representative Office of Foreign (Regional) Enterprise" issued by the administrative department for industry and commerce, or photocopy of "Approval Certificate for Resident Representative Office of Foreign Enterprise" issued by the commerce department;

c) Original and photocopy of the approval document for for-profit cultural performances issued by the cultural administrative department;

d) "Invitation Letter for Foreigners to Engage in Offshore Oil Operations in the People's Republic of China" issued by China National Offshore Oil Corporation (scanned printout or fax copy acceptable);

Applicants under 18 must provide a birth certificate and be accompanied by a guardian.

Note: For short-term work in China, a visa must be applied for according to the requirements of the "Procedures for Foreigners Entering China to Complete Short-term Work Tasks (Trial)." Please see the attachment for details.

The invitation letters mentioned above must include the following:

a) Invitee's personal information: name, gender, date of birth, etc.;

b) Invitee's visit information: purpose of visit to China, dates of arrival and departure, places to visit, relationship with the inviting unit/inviter, source of funds, etc.;

c) Inviting unit or inviter information: name of inviting unit or inviter, contact phone number, address, official seal of the unit, signature of the legal representative or inviter, etc.

Proof of Family Relationship Refers to marriage certificate, birth certificate, kinship certificate issued by a public security station, or notarized kinship certificate, etc.

Other Requirements

1. First-time travelers to China. In addition to the basic application materials, please also provide the following materials based on your personal circumstances:

a) Bank deposit certificate for the last 6 months and the bank's receipt for issuing this certificate;

b) Tax payment certificate (BIR-stamped income tax return form);

c) Employment certificate (stating income and years of service);

d) Business registration certificate (if the applicant is self-employed);

e) Professional ID, student ID, etc. (applicants may provide these based on their situation);

f) Other materials that can prove the applicant's financial status, employment, or studies (applicants may provide these based on their situation).

(Note: Children, students, homemakers, etc., who cannot provide their own bank deposit certificate and tax payment certificate may provide those of their parents or spouse, along with proof of relationship. Ôºâ


2. Former Chinese Passport: Foreign nationals of Chinese descent born in China (including Hong Kong, Macao, and Taiwan) applying for a Chinese visa for the first time must provide the original of their previously held Chinese passport (including Hong Kong, Macao, and Taiwan) and photocopies of the passport's information page and extension pages (if any). Bank deposit certificates and tax payment certificates are not required.

3. Name Change Certificate: If the name on the new passport differs from that on the old passport, an official document certifying the name change must be provided.

4. Travel to Tibet: Applicants can travel to Tibet by joining a tour group organized by a locally authorized travel agency. For independent travel to the Tibet Autonomous Region, prior contact must be made with the Tibet Tourism Bureau (Tel: 0086-891-6834313, Fax: 0086-891-6834632), and a written notice issued by this bureau must be provided.

5. Special Tourism (e.g., hiking, cycling, horseback riding, adventure, hot air ballooning, etc.): Requires a "Tourism Invitation Letter" issued by a Chinese "Authorized Tourism Unit."

Visa Application Fees (in Philippine Peso) and Processing Time ‚ÄÇ

(1) Regular Processing (Collection on the 4th working day, counting from the date of application acceptance).

(2) Express Processing (Collection on the 3rd working day), an additional fee of 1,100 Peso per visa applies.

(3) Rush Processing (Collection on the 2nd working day), an additional fee of 1,700 Peso per visa applies.

Note : If consular officers have questions during the review process and require further verification, the processing time may be extended , even if you have applied and paid for express or rush service.

Express or rush service is NOT available for F, M, X1, X2, and Z visas. Please apply well in advance.

The processing timeline is calculated from the date you submit your passport in person.

Important Notes for Visa Application

1. Application materials must be truthful and complete. The Consulate General will verify the authenticity of application materials through various channels. Those who submit forged or incomplete materials will have their applications refused, which may affect future applications.

2. Please maintain polite and civilized behavior during in-person visits. Any verbal or behavioral offense towards Consulate General staff may lead to visa refusal and affect future applications.

3. Applicants must fill out the application form truthfully, completely, and clearly. The form must be signed by the applicant personally (minors may be signed by parents or guardians, indicating the relationship).

4. Answer visa officers' questions truthfully.

5. According to relevant Chinese laws and international practice, consular officers have the right to request applicants to provide other relevant proof documents and the right to refuse a visa without stating reasons.

6. Please pay the prescribed fees at the bank. Applicants are responsible for any overpayment.

7. When collecting the visa, the applicant is responsible for checking the visa details and should raise any questions immediately.

8. "J1," "S1," "X1," and "Z" visas are valid for 3 months for a single entry. Holders must apply for a residence permit at the local public security authority within 30 days of entry.

9. Crew ("C") visas are only for the holder to perform their duties. If they need to stay in China privately after completing their duties or not for duty purposes, they must apply for the appropriate visa separately. The holder must enter and exit China together with the aircraft (train, ship). Those who cannot do so must apply for the appropriate visa separately.

10. Applicants are solely responsible for the consequences arising from the following situations; please take care to avoid them:

a) Visa application refusal due to false information on the visa form, incomplete form, or untrue declarations;

b) Visa application refusal due to non-compliant passport or photo, or failure to provide required application materials as requested by the visa officer;

c) Being denied boarding or entry due to applying too early (causing visa expiry before travel) or failing to obtain the visa in time;

d) Visa becoming invalid and unusable due to the applicant's own arrangements or third-party reasons after issuance;

e) Being denied boarding or entry due to failure to check visa entries, validity period, duration of stay, and passport validity before traveling to China;

f) Other reasons attributable to the applicant leading to the visa being unusable.
